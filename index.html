What is bitcoin?



Subjectively, Bitcoin is many things.



To some, it is a speculative monetary asset that is used to try to make huge returns.



To others, it is a fascinating, technically impressive software program made by a mysterious. anonymous online entity, which has the potential to fix some of the largest problems in our modern economy.



And to others still, it is a dangerous tool used by crooks to carry out shady criminal activity - or an energy hungry digital organism that threatens the environment.



What bitcoin is, objectively, is an open source, permissionless, peer-to-peer, monetary network protocol.



... What does that mean?



The above sentence is somewhat dense, so let's go through it one term at a time.



When something is open source, it means that it is not built by a central, owning entity (which would be closed source). Instead, contributions to its development is a collaborative effort from anyone interested enough to offer their time and skills.



Permissionless is pretty clear - no one needs any permission to use it, it is open to anyone and everyone.



Peer-to-peer means that there is no central authority that decides the state of Bitcoin. Everyone who uses is on equal footing



A monetary network is money - it's an object or set of objects that everyone agrees can be traded for items of value. The US dollar is a monetary network, as is evey other country's legal tender. But other monetary networks can be cigarettes in jail, or gold, or seashells in more primitive cultures



A protocol is a system of rules that governs the transmission of information.



So, what is Bitcoin? Bitcoin is a digital, collaboratively maintained money which cannot be controlled by any one party and is available for anyone with an internet connection to use.
